Who was Wu Zetian?

Prodigy's Ascent

Wu Zetian set off on his expedition against the grain of social practice in 624. Her early intellect led her into the palace as a concubine, laying the groundwork for her unforeseen elevation. She came from an educationally stimulating home.

Imperial Intrigue

Wu's ascent occurred in the intrigue of the court of Emperor Gaozong and his father before Emperor Taizong. Her intelligence attracted him, and she successfully negotiated the treacherous political terrain. Wu successfully overcame adversaries after Gaozong's death, contributing to her historic ascent to the throne in 690 as China's first female emperor.

Architect of Reform

Wu's power extended across imperial borders. As an avid advocate of women's rights, she encouraged education as well as granted women higher positions in the imperial administration. She had an impact on cultural accomplishments additionally; the Longmen Grottoes are proof of this. Her status as an essential component in establishing China's history was solidified through the emergence of the "Zhou Shu," a comprehensive historical chronicle.

Legacy of Wu

Wu's impact spread transcends imperial borders. As an unwavering advocate for women's rights, she encouraged education and provided women with higher positions in the imperial administration. She had a profound effect on artistic accomplishments as well; the Longmen Grottoes are proof of this. Her importance as a key player in influencing Chinese history was cemented with the creation of the "Zhou Shu," an extensive historical chronicle.
